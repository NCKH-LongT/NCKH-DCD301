# Paper 12 Summary

## 12. Smart Sustainable Greenhouses Utilizing Microcontroller and IOT in the GCC Countries; Energy Requirements & Economical Analyses Study for a Concept Model in the State of Qatar

## Citation

**Title:** Smart Sustainable Greenhouses Utilizing Microcontroller and IOT in the GCC Countries; Energy Requirements & Economical Analyses Study for a Concept Model in the State of Qatar  
**Authors:** Salem Al-Naemi, Awni Al-Otoom  
**Year:** 2023  
**Source:** Results in Engineering, Elsevier  
**DOI/Link:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.rineng.2023.100824  
**Link:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2590123023000166

## Problem

The paper addresses the challenge of building sustainable greenhouse systems in hot and dry regions such as GCC countries. Greenhouses in these areas require large amounts of energy and water to maintain suitable growing conditions.

Traditional greenhouse systems may be costly and inefficient if they do not use automated monitoring, IoT control, renewable energy, and water management. Therefore, the paper proposes and analyzes a smart sustainable greenhouse concept model.

## Method

The paper proposes a smart greenhouse model using microcontrollers and IoT-based control.

The system focuses on monitoring and controlling important greenhouse parameters such as:

- Temperature
- Humidity
- Irrigation
- Energy usage
- Water management

The paper also analyzes energy requirements and economic feasibility. It considers renewable energy, especially photovoltaic power, and water recycling to improve sustainability.

## Dataset

This paper does not use a machine learning dataset.

Instead, it uses system design parameters, environmental requirements, greenhouse energy requirements, and economic analysis data for the greenhouse concept model.

## Evaluation

The paper evaluates the proposed greenhouse model using:

- Energy requirement analysis
- Economic feasibility analysis
- Water consumption analysis
- Renewable energy capacity estimation
- Greenhouse operation cost analysis
- Practical feasibility of IoT-based control

## Results

The paper shows that a smart greenhouse using microcontrollers, IoT, renewable energy, and water recycling can improve sustainability and reduce operational cost.

The results indicate that photovoltaic power with storage can support greenhouse energy needs, while water recycling can reduce water consumption.

The study supports the feasibility of IoT-based smart greenhouse systems in hot and dry regions.

## Limitations

The paper focuses on greenhouse system design and sustainability analysis, not BCD arithmetic or FPGA-based decimal processing.

It does not discuss hardware-level sensor data validation, BCD error detection, or Agentic RAG decision support.

The study is also specific to GCC/Qatar conditions, so the results may need adaptation for other regions or greenhouse environments.

## Relevance to Our Topic

This paper is relevant to the topic **BCD Arithmetic Error Detection for Reliable Sensor Data Processing in AIoT Smart Greenhouse Systems** because it provides the smart greenhouse application context.

It helps explain why greenhouse systems need reliable sensor monitoring and automatic control. Accurate sensor data is important for controlling temperature, humidity, irrigation, and energy usage.

## Possible Improvement

Our group can improve the smart greenhouse model by adding a reliable sensor data processing module based on **BCD arithmetic error detection**.

Possible improvements include:

- Validating decimal sensor values before control decisions
- Detecting invalid BCD codes from sensor readings
- Detecting abnormal or out-of-range greenhouse values
- Adding data quality flags before irrigation or climate control
- Using Agentic RAG to explain greenhouse recommendations based on validated data
