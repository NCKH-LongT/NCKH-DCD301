# Paper 11 Summary

## 11. Artificial Intelligence of Things (AIoT) for Smart Agriculture: A Review of Architectures, Technologies and Solutions

## Citation

**Title:** Artificial Intelligence of Things (AIoT) for Smart Agriculture: A Review of Architectures, Technologies and Solutions  
**Authors:** Dalhatu Muhammed, Ehsan Ahvar, Shohreh Ahvar, Maria Trocan, Marie-José Montpetit, Reza Ehsani  
**Year:** 2024  
**Source:** Journal of Network and Computer Applications, Elsevier  
**DOI/Link:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jnca.2024.103905  
**Link:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1084804524000821

## Problem

The paper addresses the need for intelligent and automated systems in modern agriculture. Traditional agriculture often depends on manual observation and decision-making, which can be inefficient, slow, and inaccurate.

Smart agriculture requires reliable sensing, communication, data processing, artificial intelligence, and decision support. However, existing studies on AIoT in agriculture are often fragmented across different technologies and applications. Therefore, the paper reviews AIoT architectures, technologies, and solutions for smart agriculture.

## Method

The paper uses a review-based methodology. It surveys existing research on Artificial Intelligence of Things (AIoT) in smart agriculture.

The review covers several components of AIoT systems, including:

- IoT sensors
- Communication technologies
- Edge/cloud computing
- Artificial intelligence and machine learning
- Smart agriculture applications
- Decision-support systems
- System architectures and future challenges

## Dataset

This paper does not use a single experimental dataset because it is a review paper.

Instead, it analyzes and summarizes findings from existing studies related to AIoT, IoT, artificial intelligence, and smart agriculture applications.

## Evaluation

The paper evaluates previous research qualitatively by comparing AIoT architectures, technologies, solutions, and application domains.

The evaluation focuses on:

- AIoT system architecture
- Application areas in smart agriculture
- Advantages and challenges of AIoT technologies
- Sensor and communication requirements
- Research gaps and future directions

## Results

The paper shows that AIoT can improve smart agriculture by combining sensor-based monitoring with AI-based analysis and decision-making.

AIoT can support applications such as:

- Crop monitoring
- Soil and water management
- Pest and disease detection
- Greenhouse automation
- Yield prediction
- Resource optimization
- Agricultural decision support

The paper also highlights that reliable sensor data and intelligent processing are essential for effective AIoT systems.

## Limitations

The paper is a review paper, so it does not provide a new hardware design, algorithm, or experimental system.

It does not focus specifically on BCD arithmetic, FPGA implementation, or arithmetic error detection. Therefore, additional specialized papers are needed to support the hardware arithmetic part of the project.

## Relevance to Our Topic

This paper is relevant to the topic **BCD Arithmetic Error Detection for Reliable Sensor Data Processing in AIoT Smart Greenhouse Systems** because it provides the overall AIoT smart agriculture background.

It helps explain why smart greenhouse systems need sensors, AI models, data processing, and decision support. It also supports the idea that reliable sensor data is important before generating recommendations or automatic control actions.

## Possible Improvement

Our group can extend the AIoT architecture discussed in this paper by adding a specific **BCD arithmetic error detection layer** for sensor data validation.

Possible improvements include:

- Adding BCD validation before AI-based analysis
- Detecting invalid decimal sensor values
