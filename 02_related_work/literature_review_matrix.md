# Literature Review Matrix — Group 7 (Week 2)

| Paper | Year | Venue | Problem | Method | Dataset/System | Limitation | Relation to our work |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **[IoT/AIoT]** Artificial Intelligence of Things (AIoT) for Smart Agriculture: A Review of Architectures, Technologies and Solutions (Dalhatu Muhammed et al.) | 2023 | Journal of Network and Computer Applications | Systematizing AIoT architectures in agriculture and integrating AI and IoT data. | Reviewing AIoT architectures, Federated Learning, and edge/cloud computing patterns. | General AIoT agricultural architecture | Broad survey with limited experimental and concrete benchmark evidence. | Provides the foundational theory to design the AIoT architecture for our smart greenhouse. |
| **[IoT/AIoT]** Smart Sensors and Smart Data for Precision Agriculture: A Review (Abdellatif Soussi et al.) | 2024 | Sensors | The role of smart sensors and data pipelines in precision agriculture. | Reviewing sensor types, wireless sensor network (WSN) architectures, and communication protocols. | Agricultural data acquisition system | Focuses heavily on sensor hardware, with less emphasis on downstream machine learning pipelines. | Helps design the input sensor list and establish physical sensor fault scenarios. |
| **[IoT/AIoT]** Electrochemical Sensors for Sustainable Precision Agriculture (Min-Yeong Kim & Kyu Hwan Lee) | 2022 | Frontiers in Chemistry | Electrochemical sensors for in-situ nutrient and biochemical monitoring in precision agriculture. | Synthesizing electrochemical sensor methods, ion-selective electrodes (ISE), and plant-wearable sensors. | In-situ farm chemical sensing | Mostly laboratory and bench-scale results; dynamic integration at farm scale remains limited. | Serves as a reference for advanced sensor modalities beyond standard physical parameters. |
| **[IoT/AIoT]** IoT-based smart irrigation management system to enhance agricultural water security (Abdennabi Morchid et al.) | 2024 | Results in Engineering | Smart irrigation and water security management using embedded systems and cloud telemetry. | Designing embedded hardware, cloud telemetry via ThingsBoard, and threshold-based static control. | ThingsBoard Cloud system | Small-scale testing; relies on static threshold-based rules, lacking dynamic reasoning. | Used as a baseline system (Rule-based Baseline) for smart greenhouse irrigation control. |
| **[RAG/LLM]** Retrieval-Augmented Generation for Knowledge-Intensive NLP Tasks (Lewis et al.) | 2020 | NeurIPS | Proposing RAG to combine parametric memory (LLM) and non-parametric memory (Vector DB). | Dense Passage Retrieval (DPR) combined with BART generator; RAG-Sequence and RAG-Token models. | Wikipedia index / Open QA datasets | Highly dependent on retriever quality; high computational cost for indexing. | The foundational framework to build our RAG pipeline for agricultural knowledge retrieval. |
| **[RAG/LLM]** A Survey on Retrieval-Augmented Text Generation for Large Language Models (Huang & Huang) | 2024 | arXiv | Systematizing the development phases of RAG for LLMs and outlining evaluation criteria. | Summarizing RAG into four stages: Pre-retrieval, Retrieval, Post-retrieval, and Generation. | Theoretical RAG survey framework | Does not detail physical control scenarios or real-time telemetry data integration. | Guides the selection of RAG optimization techniques (e.g., reranking, optimal chunking). |
| **[RAG/LLM]** AgriIR: A Scalable Framework for Domain-Specific Knowledge Retrieval (Seal et al.) | 2026 | ACM | Proposing a highly efficient, modular, and cost-effective agricultural RAG framework. | Implementing multi-threaded sub-query refinement, structured retrieval, and deterministic citation. | AgriIR / Test on 191 complex agricultural queries | Dependent on initial search keywords; empirical testing localized to Indian agricultural contexts. | Demonstrates how to design domain-specific RAG systems with precise, deterministic citations. |
| **[RAG/LLM]** Explainable AI with LLMs: From Black Box to Transparent Decisions (Bilal et al.) | 2025 | arXiv | Surveying methods for explaining AI models (XAI) using large language models. | Categorizing LLM-based XAI into three groups: post-hoc, intrinsic, and human-centered; proposing evaluation metrics. | XAI literature survey | Lacks standardized, automated, and quantitative evaluation criteria. | Shapes the structured JSON output format for recommendations and explanations in our system. |
| **[Data Quality]** A Systematic Review of Data Quality in IoT Sensor Networks (Zhang et al.) | 2023 | IEEE Internet of Things Journal | Detecting and resolving sensor data quality issues (noise, loss, outliers) at the IoT edge. | Proposing statistical and machine learning methods for data imputation and anomaly detection. | IoT Sensor Benchmark Dataset | Edge-side computational overhead is high when handling massive data volumes. | Provides the mathematical foundation to build the Data Quality Module (missing/outlier checks). |
| **[Data Quality]** Anomaly and Fault Detection in Multi-Sensor Environments for Smart Agriculture (Al-Omari et al.) | 2024 | Computers and Electronics in Agriculture | Detecting sensor faults such as stuck readings, calibration drift, and cross-sensor conflicts. | Applying adaptive Kalman filtering and spatio-temporal correlation analysis. | Simulated greenhouse datasets | Requires complex initial calibration and lacks dynamic auto-calibration. | Directly applied to simulate and detect Sensor Fault and Conflicting Sensor scenarios. |
| **[Data Quality]** Spatio-Temporal Cross-Sensor Data Quality Assessment in Precision Agriculture (Silva et al.) | 2024 | Sensors | Assessing reliability and detecting conflicts between sensors measuring redundant or related parameters. | Designing cross-sensor consistency checks based on physical constraints (e.g., temperature vs. humidity). | Farm-scale sensor networks | High false alarm rates during sudden microclimate fluctuations. | Used to build our cross-sensor conflict detection algorithms and calculate data quality scores. |
| **[Agent/XAI]** Causal Explanations for Sequential Decision-Making in Multi-Agent Systems (Bilal et al. - CEMA) | 2025 | AAAI | AI explanations lack causal reasoning and are difficult for non-expert human operators to interpret. | CEMA framework: Roll-back time -> Probabilistic counterfactual simulation -> Causal effect size calculation. | HEADD Dataset / IGP2 Driving System | Highly dependent on the accuracy of the probabilistic forward simulator. | Guides the design of our causal explanation generator based on the smart greenhouse's FSM states. |
| **[Agent/XAI]** Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) for Fintech Agent Systems: Framework and Benchmark (Wang et al.) | 2024 | ACM | Applying orchestrating RAG systems for agents handling complex sequential reasoning tasks. | Designing a stateful retrieval orchestration framework and benchmark tasks. | Fintech Agent Benchmark | Optimized specifically for financial text, making direct hardware deployment challenging. | Informs our stateful Agentic RAG design using LangGraph to maintain operational states. |
| **[Agent/XAI]** A Survey of Multi-Agent Deep Reinforcement Learning for Complex Sequential Control (Li et al.) | 2024 | IEEE TNNLS | Coordination and credit assignment among multiple agents in sequential control problems. | Centralized Training, Decentralized Execution (CTDE) framework with Transformer-based state modeling. | Industrial control simulator | Large action spaces cause high execution latency, limiting edge deployment. | Referenced to design the multi-agent control coordination for our smart greenhouse. |
| **[Agent/XAI]** ReAct: Synergizing Reasoning and Acting in Language Models (Yao et al.) | 2023 | ICLR | LLMs lack dynamic alignment between logical reasoning and concrete action execution. | ReAct methodology: Alternating between Thought (Reasoning) -> Action -> Observation cycles. | HotpotQA / Fever / WebShop | Increases API consumption costs and overall execution latency. | Used in our Agentic Reasoning workflow to sequentially call tools (Data Quality and RAG). |
